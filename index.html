I am in my third year of a Bachelor of Exercise and Sport Science at Swinburne University, working toward a career in physiotherapy. Exercise science was not my original area of study. I first completed a course in Screen Production, gaining skills in photography, editing, and sound recording, then spent several years working in film independently. I directed music videos, short films, and documentaries, and acted in a few productions. Most recently I made a short film on a professional bodybuilder, focusing on discipline, performance, and the mindset of an athlete.

"My interest in physiotherapy stems from my mum. She is a physio, and I have witnessed the work she does for people's wellbeing throughout my life. It was the whole package of treatment and relational skills that left a mark on me."

I have been fortunate to have several experiences outside of Australia. I completed mission trips to Indonesia and Sri Lanka, shadowing physiotherapists and doctors in communities with extremely limited resources. I will never forget the opportunity to document a cleft lip and palate surgery in Manado. These trips gave me a wider view of healthcare and effectively bridged the gap between my studies and real life. I am currently planning my third trip to India.

I also work as a Client Advisor at Bottega Veneta. This very different working environment has been invaluable in helping me develop interpersonal communication, attention to detail, and the ability to genuinely connect with people. Those skills are more transferable to healthcare than I would have expected.

I am currently on placement at Kieser Sandringham, working with physiotherapists, exercise physiologists, and exercise scientists. This hands-on experience has given me a clear view of how theory and practice integrate in a clinical setting. Long term, I want to be a physiotherapist but do not want to lose the creative side of what I have done. I want to combine both, using media to make health and rehabilitation more accessible and easier to understand for people.
