I am a third-year Bachelor of Exercise and Sport Science student at Swinburne University of Technology, working toward a career in physiotherapy.

Before exercise science, I studied Screen Production at Swinburne, completing units in photography, film editing, and sound recording. I then pursued an independent career in film — directing music videos, short films, documentaries, and acting in various productions. Most recently I produced a short film following a professional bodybuilder, exploring human performance, discipline, and athlete psychology.

"My mother is a physiotherapist. Watching her work — the way she listens, diagnoses, and genuinely changes people's lives — is the greatest inspiration for my career."

I have completed mission trips to Indonesia and Sri Lanka, shadowing physiotherapists and doctors in underserved communities. In Manado, I documented a cleft lip and palate surgery. These experiences gave me a real-world perspective on healthcare that no classroom can replicate. I am planning a third trip to India.

I currently work as a Client Advisor at Bottega Veneta, one of the world's leading luxury fashion houses. This role demands precision in communication, deep client relationship management, and the ability to connect with people from every background — skills that transfer directly into patient-facing healthcare.

I am on clinical placement at Kieser Sandringham, working alongside physiotherapists, exercise physiologists, and exercise scientists. My long-term goal is to become a Physiotherapist, combining clinical practice with media to make health education more accessible to those who need it most.
